Careers That Use This Math

Every skill in Math 8 and Math 1 is directly used in real careers. Here's where the curriculum shows up in the real world:

Linear equations & slope: Civil engineers calculate road grades, economists model supply and demand, data scientists build trend lines.
Exponential functions: Epidemiologists model disease spread, finance professionals calculate compound interest, biologists study population growth.
Pythagorean theorem: Architects, carpenters, surveyors, and GPS systems all use distance in the coordinate plane.
Statistics & regression: Sports analysts, medical researchers, social scientists, and machine learning engineers work with correlation and regression every day.
Quadratics: Physicists model projectile motion, game designers program realistic physics, engineers design parabolic bridges and satellite dishes.
Transformations: Computer graphics engineers, animators, and robotics programmers use rotation, translation, and reflection matrices constantly.
About This Toolkit

What This Is

The Math Teaching Toolkit is a free, open-source web application built for NC Math 8 and Math 1 teachers. It contains deep content guides, interactive classroom tools, printable materials, student-facing practice, and curated resources — all in one place, with no login required.

It is not a curriculum. It is a layer of structure and tooling on top of whatever curriculum your school uses — designed around three real problems teachers face: retention (making content stick), differentiation (reaching mixed-ability classrooms), and pacing (finishing on time).

The Pedagogical Framework
🔄

NC2ML — Launch–Explore–Discuss

The "Sticky Math Block" in this toolkit is the NC2ML Launch–Explore–Discuss model with retrieval practice (Spiral Do-Now) and formative assessment (Exit Ticket) added. NC Collaborative for Mathematics Learning.

nc2ml.org
🧠

Retrieval Practice & Spaced Practice

The Spiral Do-Now is built on retrieval practice research — recalling information from memory is more effective for long-term retention than re-reading or re-studying. Free research summary available here.

retrievalpractice.org
💬

5 Practices for Orchestrating Discussion

Smith & Stein's framework for productive mathematical discussion: Anticipate, Monitor, Select, Sequence, Connect. The Explore phase and lesson plan template in this toolkit are built around this model.

nctm.org
🔢

Concrete–Representational–Abstract (CRA)

The instructional sequence used throughout the Math 8 and Math 1 content pages — starting with physical/concrete models, moving to pictures, then to abstract notation. Especially effective for algebra concepts.

iste.org

How to Use This Toolkit
If you're a teacher getting started

Read the home page for the Sticky Math Block overview, then go to your course (Math 8 or Math 1) for domain-by-domain strategies. Use the Lesson Plan Template for planning and the Block Timer to run the structure live in class.

If you're a student

Start with Homework Practice for domain-organized problem sets and instant answer-checking. Use the Reference Cards as a study guide. The Video Library has concept explanations for every major topic.

If you're a parent or guardian

The Homework Practice page shows the problems your student is working on with full answers. The Reference Cards give you the key formulas. The Video Library has Khan Academy links for every topic.

If you're an educator from outside NC

The NC Standard Course of Study is closely aligned to CCSS. Most content, strategies, and tools on this site apply directly to any 8th grade and Algebra 1 classroom. NC-specific standard codes are listed on every resource — match them to your state's standards as needed.

Resource links are curated for quality and relevance. External sites are not affiliated with or endorsed by this toolkit. NC standard codes and EOG/EOC references reflect the current NC Standard Course of Study — verify against current NCDPI documents for the most up-to-date specifications. This toolkit is open and free to use by anyone.
