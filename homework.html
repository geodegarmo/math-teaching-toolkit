NC.8.EE.1–8

Set 1 — Expressions & Equations

1
Core
Simplify: (x³)² · x⁴
x¹⁰ — use the power rule first: (x³)² = x⁶, then product rule: x⁶ · x⁴ = x¹⁰
2
Core
Write 0.000056 in scientific notation.
5.6 × 10⁻⁵ — count 5 places left to put the decimal after the 5
3
Core
Solve: 4x + 7 = 31
x = 6 — subtract 7: 4x = 24; divide by 4
4
Core
Solve: 3(2x − 1) = x + 12
x = 3 — distribute: 6x − 3 = x + 12; subtract x: 5x = 15; x = 3
5
Core
Solve: x/4 − 2 = 6
x = 32 — add 2: x/4 = 8; multiply by 4
6
Core
Solve the system: y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 7
(2, 5) — set equal: 2x + 1 = −x + 7; 3x = 6; x = 2; y = 5
7
Core
Calculate: (3 × 10⁴) × (2 × 10³). Write in proper scientific notation.
6 × 10⁷ — multiply coefficients: 3 × 2 = 6; add exponents: 4 + 3 = 7
8
Challenge
Solve: 2(x + 3) − 5 = 2x + 1. Classify as one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions. Explain why.
No solution — distribute: 2x + 6 − 5 = 2x + 1 → 2x + 1 = 2x + 1 → 0 = 0. Wait — that's infinitely many! Both sides simplify to 2x + 1 = 2x + 1, which is always true. Infinitely many solutions.
9
Challenge
Two numbers have a sum of 100. One is 4 times the other. Write and solve a system of equations to find both numbers.
20 and 80 — let x + y = 100 and y = 4x; substitute: x + 4x = 100; 5x = 100; x = 20, y = 80

NC.8.F.1–5

Set 2 — Functions

1
Core
Does this mapping show a function? Input: {1, 2, 3}, Output: {1→5, 2→7, 2→9, 3→11}. Explain.
No — input 2 maps to two different outputs (7 and 9). A function must have exactly one output per input.
2
Core
A table shows x: 0, 1, 2, 3 and y: 3, 7, 11, 15. Find the rate of change and write an equation.
Rate of change = 4 (y increases by 4 each time x increases by 1); equation: y = 4x + 3
3
Core
Write the equation of a line with slope −3 and y-intercept 7.
y = −3x + 7
4
Core
A line passes through (0, −2) and (5, 8). Find the slope and write the equation.
m = (8−(−2))/(5−0) = 10/5 = 2; equation: y = 2x − 2
5
Core
Classify the slope: a road drops 4 feet for every 100 feet of horizontal distance. Positive, negative, zero, or undefined?
Negative — the road falls as horizontal distance increases
6
Core
For f(x) = 5x − 3, evaluate f(0), f(2), and f(−1).
f(0) = −3; f(2) = 7; f(−1) = −8
7
Challenge
Compare f(x) = 4x + 1 (equation) to g(x) shown in the table: x: 0→3, 1→7, 2→11. Which function has the greater y-intercept? Which has the greater rate of change?
f has y-intercept 1; g has y-intercept 3. g has greater y-intercept. Rate of change: f has slope 4; g has slope 4 (7−3=4 per step). Equal rates of change — both slope 4.
8
Challenge
A linear function passes through (2, 9) and (6, 17). Write the equation. Then find the x-intercept.
m = (17−9)/(6−2) = 8/4 = 2; using (2,9): 9 = 2(2)+b → b = 5; y = 2x + 5. x-intercept: 0 = 2x+5 → x = −2.5

NC.8.G.1–9

Set 3 — Geometry

1
Core
A right triangle has legs of 6 cm and 8 cm. Find the hypotenuse.
10 cm — a² + b² = c²; 36 + 64 = 100; c = √100 = 10
2
Core
Point A(3, −1) is reflected over the x-axis. What are the new coordinates?
A'(3, 1) — reflecting over x-axis negates the y-coordinate: (x, −y)
3
Core
Translate point B(−2, 4) by (h, k) = (5, −3). Find B'.
B'(3, 1) — add h to x: −2+5=3; add k to y: 4+(−3)=1
4
Core
Rotate point C(2, 5) 90° counterclockwise about the origin.
C'(−5, 2) — rule for 90° CCW: (x, y) → (−y, x)
5
Core
Do the sides 7, 24, 25 form a right triangle? Show your check.
Yes — 7² + 24² = 49 + 576 = 625 = 25². The equation a² + b² = c² holds.
6
Core
Find the volume of a cone with radius 6 cm and height 9 cm. Leave answer in terms of π.
108π cm³ — V = ⅓πr²h = ⅓π(36)(9) = 108π
7
Core
A dilation with scale factor k = 2 is applied to a triangle with area 15 cm². What is the area of the image?
60 cm² — dilation by k scales lengths by k and area by k²; 15 × 4 = 60
8
Challenge
Find the distance between points P(1, 2) and Q(7, 10). Round to the nearest tenth.
10 — d = √((7−1)² + (10−2)²) = √(36+64) = √100 = 10
9
Challenge
A sphere has a volume of 288π cm³. Find its radius.
r = 6 cm — V = ⁴⁄₃πr³; 288π = ⁴⁄₃πr³; r³ = 216; r = 6

NC.8.SP.1–4

Set 4 — Statistics & Data

1
Core
A scatter plot shows that as hours of studying increase, test scores increase. Describe the association (direction and strength).
Positive association — as x (hours) increases, y (scores) increases. Strength depends on how tightly clustered the points are around the trend line.
2
Core
The correlation coefficient r = −0.87. Describe the association.
Strong negative association — r is close to −1, meaning as x increases, y decreases, and the relationship is fairly linear and tight
3
Core
A line of best fit is ŷ = 3x + 12. Predict the y-value when x = 8.
36 — ŷ = 3(8) + 12 = 24 + 12 = 36
4
Core
A two-way table: 40 total students; 25 prefer pizza, 15 prefer tacos; 18 of the pizza-preferrers are in 8th grade. What fraction of pizza-preferrers are in 8th grade?
18/25 = 0.72 or 72%
5
Core
Explain in one sentence the difference between correlation and causation. Give an example of each.
Correlation means two variables are related; causation means one directly causes the other. Example: Ice cream sales and drowning rates are correlated (both rise in summer) but ice cream does not cause drowning.
6
Core
Identify any outliers and describe their effect on the line of best fit: data cluster around (2,5) to (10,30), but one point is at (2, 95).
The point (2, 95) is an outlier. It would pull the line of best fit upward and reduce the slope, making predictions less accurate for the rest of the data.
7
Challenge
The line of best fit for a study on exercise (hours/week) and resting heart rate (bpm) is ŷ = −2.5x + 80. Interpret the slope and y-intercept in context.
Slope: for each additional hour of exercise per week, resting heart rate decreases by about 2.5 bpm on average. Y-intercept: a person who exercises 0 hours/week has a predicted resting heart rate of 80 bpm.

NC.M1.A-SSE · A-CED · A-REI

Set 1 — Linear Algebra & Systems

1
Core
Solve: 5x − 2 = 3x + 8
x = 5 — subtract 3x: 2x − 2 = 8; add 2: 2x = 10; x = 5
2
Core
Solve: 2(y + 4) − 3 = 5y − 1
y = 2 — distribute: 2y + 8 − 3 = 5y − 1; 2y + 5 = 5y − 1; 6 = 3y; y = 2
3
Core
Solve the system: 2x + y = 7 and x − y = 2
(3, 1) — add the equations: 3x = 9; x = 3; substitute: 3 − y = 2; y = 1
4
Core
Simplify: 4x² − 3x + 2x² + 7x − 5
6x² + 4x − 5 — combine like terms: (4+2)x² + (−3+7)x − 5
5
Core
Factor: x² + 5x + 6
(x + 2)(x + 3) — find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5: 2 and 3
6
Core
Write and solve an inequality: "A number decreased by 4 is at most 11." Graph the solution set.
x − 4 ≤ 11; x ≤ 15. Graph: closed circle at 15, shaded to the left.
7
Challenge
Solve by elimination: 3x + 2y = 16 and x − 2y = 0. Then verify your solution.
(4, 2) — add equations: 4x = 16; x = 4; substitute: 4 − 2y = 0; y = 2. Check: 3(4)+2(2) = 16 ✓; 4−2(2) = 0 ✓
8
Challenge
The sum of two numbers is 45. The larger number is 9 more than twice the smaller. Find both numbers.
12 and 33 — let s = smaller; s + (2s + 9) = 45; 3s = 36; s = 12; larger = 33. Check: 12 + 33 = 45 ✓; 33 = 2(12)+9 ✓

NC.M1.A-SSE · A-REI · F-IF

Set 2 — Quadratics

1
Core
Solve by factoring: x² − 7x + 12 = 0
x = 3 or x = 4 — factor: (x−3)(x−4) = 0; set each factor to zero
2
Core
Use the quadratic formula to solve: 2x² + 3x − 2 = 0
x = ½ or x = −2 — formula: x = (−3 ± √(9+16))/4 = (−3 ± 5)/4
3
Core
Find the vertex of y = x² − 6x + 5.
(3, −4) — axis of symmetry x = −b/2a = 6/2 = 3; y(3) = 9 − 18 + 5 = −4
4
Core
Does y = −3x² + 4x − 1 open up or down? Does it have a maximum or minimum?
Opens down (a = −3 < 0); has a maximum value at the vertex
5
Core
Expand and simplify: (x + 5)(x − 3)
x² + 2x − 15 — FOIL: x² − 3x + 5x − 15
6
Core
Factor completely: 2x² − 8
2(x+2)(x−2) — factor out GCF 2: 2(x²−4); difference of squares: 2(x+2)(x−2)
7
Challenge
The height of a ball (in feet) is h(t) = −16t² + 48t + 4. At what time(s) does the ball reach a height of 40 feet?
Set −16t² + 48t + 4 = 40 → −16t² + 48t − 36 = 0 → 4t² − 12t + 9 = 0 → (2t−3)² = 0 → t = 1.5 sec (once, at the vertex)
8
Challenge
Write a quadratic in vertex form with vertex (2, −5) that opens upward. Then convert it to standard form.
Vertex form: y = (x−2)² − 5; standard form: y = x² − 4x + 4 − 5 = x² − 4x − 1

NC.M1.F-IF · F-LE

Set 3 — Function Families

1
Core
Identify the function family: x: 0, 1, 2, 3 | y: 1, 3, 9, 27. Write the equation.
Exponential — each y-value is multiplied by 3; equation: y = 1 · 3ˣ = 3ˣ
2
Core
A linear function has f(0) = 6 and f(3) = 15. Write the equation.
y = 3x + 6 — slope = (15−6)/(3−0) = 3; y-intercept = f(0) = 6
3
Core
A population starts at 2,000 and grows 5% per year. Write the exponential function for the population P after t years.
P(t) = 2000(1.05)ᵗ — general form f(t) = a · bᵗ; a = 2000 (initial); b = 1 + 0.05 = 1.05 (growth factor)
4
Core
The second differences of a sequence are constant at 4. What function family does it belong to?
Quadratic — constant first differences → linear; constant second differences → quadratic
5
Core
A car depreciates 12% per year. It was worth $25,000 new. Write the function and find its value after 4 years.
V(t) = 25000(0.88)ᵗ — b = 1 − 0.12 = 0.88 (decay); V(4) = 25000(0.88)⁴ ≈ $14,956
6
Core
For f(x) = 3x² − 12x + 7, find the axis of symmetry and the vertex.
Axis of symmetry: x = −(−12)/(2·3) = 2; vertex: f(2) = 12 − 24 + 7 = −5; vertex = (2, −5)
7
Challenge
A table shows: x: 0→5, 1→6, 2→7.2, 3→8.64. Identify the family, write the equation, and find the y-value when x = 5 (round to nearest hundredth).
Exponential — ratio: 6/5 = 1.2 each time. Equation: y = 5(1.2)ˣ. At x = 5: y = 5(1.2)⁵ = 5(2.48832) ≈ 12.44

NC.M1.S-ID

Set 4 — Statistics & Data Analysis

1
Core
The correlation coefficient r = 0.93. Describe what this means about the relationship between two variables.
Strong positive linear association — as one variable increases, the other tends to increase, and the data points cluster closely around the line of best fit
2
Core
Data set: 12, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 80. Find the mean and median. Which better describes the center? Why?
Mean = (12+14+15+16+17+18+80)/7 = 172/7 ≈ 24.6; Median = 16. Median better describes center — the outlier (80) inflates the mean significantly.
3
Core
The LSRL for a data set is ŷ = 4.5x + 22. Predict y when x = 6.
49 — ŷ = 4.5(6) + 22 = 27 + 22 = 49
4
Core
r² = 0.74 for a study on hours of sleep (x) and reaction time (y). What does this mean?
74% of the variation in reaction time is explained by the linear relationship with hours of sleep. The remaining 26% is due to other factors.
5
Core
A box plot shows: min = 10, Q1 = 18, median = 25, Q3 = 34, max = 50. Calculate the IQR and identify any possible outliers.
IQR = Q3 − Q1 = 34 − 18 = 16. Outlier fence: Q3 + 1.5(16) = 58; Q1 − 1.5(16) = −6. No data is outside [−6, 58], so no outliers.
6
Challenge
A study finds r = −0.85 between screen time (hours/day) and GPA. A student claims "phones cause bad grades." Is this a valid conclusion? Explain.
Not necessarily — r = −0.85 shows strong negative correlation but not causation. There may be lurking variables (e.g., students with more screen time may also study less, sleep less, etc.). Correlation does not imply causation.
7
Challenge
The LSRL for exercise (hours/week) and resting heart rate (bpm) is ŷ = −3.1x + 82. Interpret the slope and predict the heart rate for someone who exercises 5 hours/week.
Slope: heart rate decreases by about 3.1 bpm for each additional hour of exercise per week. Prediction: ŷ = −3.1(5) + 82 = −15.5 + 82 = 66.5 bpm
