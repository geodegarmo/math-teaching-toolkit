Pacing & Unit Calendar

Pacing problems are usually planning problems. A predictable structure and a few decision rules remove most of the nightly guesswork — and the test weights tell you exactly where the time should go.

Decision Rules

Let test weights set your time budget

Pacing

Spend instructional time roughly in proportion to EOG/EOC domain weights. If Number/EE is 30% of the Math 8 EOG and Statistics is 20%, EE deserves 50% more time. Don't over-invest weeks in a lightly weighted topic because it's your favorite or it seems more engaging.

Math 8 rough day allocation (180-day year, subtract review/assessment days):
~45 days: Number / Expressions & Equations (30%)
~38 days: Functions (25%)
~38 days: Geometry (25%)
~30 days: Statistics & Probability (20%)
~29 days: flex, review, assessments

Math 1 rough day allocation:
~54 days: Algebra (36–40%)
~48 days: Functions (32–36%)
~27 days: Statistics (18–20%)
~12 days: Geometry (8–12%)
~39 days: flex, review, assessments

The exit-ticket decision rule

PacingDifferentiation

Make it a rule, not a gut call — take the guesswork out of "did they get it."

If > ⅓ of exit tickets are "not yet": Open tomorrow with a targeted reteach for that group (10 min, pull them to a table) while the rest extend with a may-do. Then continue with the class.

If ≤ ⅓ are "not yet": Move the class forward. Pull the "not yet" students into a small group during Explore tomorrow. Their Do-Now spiral should include a problem from this concept for the next two weeks.

Never sacrifice the majority's pacing for the minority's reteach — that's what the spiral Do-Now is for. The reteach is a small-group intervention, not a whole-class restart.

Build flex days into every unit

Pacing

Plan ~1 buffer day per 5–6 instructional days. Snow days, assemblies, fire drills, and "they didn't get it" days happen. A unit with no slack ends in a rushed mess the day before the unit test.

Flex days are not wasted days. A well-placed flex day becomes: extra practice on the hardest concept of the unit, student presentations or justifications, a rich problem that connects the unit to prior learning, or a structured review activity. Only upgrade to review if students genuinely don't need more time.

"Minimum viable lesson" for shortened periods

Pacing

For 25-minute periods (assemblies, fire drills, testing days): Spiral Do-Now + one rich problem + exit ticket. The block compresses cleanly. Never use a shortened period as a free day — even 20 minutes of focused retrieval practice is better than none.
